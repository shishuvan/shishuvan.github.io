Rules And Regulations

GENERAL

  • Children need to be in school by 8.50 a.m. (Sharp). If a child comes in later than 8.50 am in pre-primary more than twice in a month, then (s)he will be sent back home.
  • Use of the school bus is mandatory for everyone studying in Nursery to Std. VIII, except for those who can walk to school. Use of personal vehicles to drop the students is not allowed due to heavy traffic in the lane.
  • Children are not allowed to:
    • Wear or bring valuables to the school.
    • carry money to school.
    • Apply Mehendi (Except with prior permission), nail polish and/or tattoo.(Parents of the children found with nails polish will be called to school with the nail polish remover to remove it. )
    • Children found with hair colour application will not be allowed to attend the school for a week.
  • Gifts and cash are strictly not to be given to any staff member including bus attendants, on any occasion.
  • Personal hygiene is important –
    • Please ensure that your child's nails are clipped. After two warnings the parent would to called to school to do the needful.
    • Only short cropped hair is allowed for boys. Mushroom cuts and spiked hair cuts (fancy hair cuts) are not allowed.
    • For girls, hair should be neatly tied with black plain elastic bands or black clips. Hair till neck length should be tied into ponytail(s) and hair beyond the neck length should be neatly plaited.
    • A handkerchief should be sent in the child's pocket.
  • The school is not responsible if children misplace any of their belongings.
  • If you wish to celebrate your child's birthday in class, please note that you may send only an eggless cake or an Indian sweet with prior intimation. Cakes should be handed over to the school office by 10.15 a.m. (As parents are not allowed into classroom). No return gifts and sweets/chocolates can be sent for your child's classmates. Play school & Nursery parents can take photographs of the celebration. Jr.Kg. onwards, parents will not be allowed inside the class.
  • The school strongly discourages tuition and private classes for the children. The school expects you to co-operate in this regard.
  • A fine will be charged on late payment of school & bus fees.
  • A fine will also be charged for late pick up of children after 12.15 p.m. at Rs.25/- in the school office.
    • UNIFORM
  • School uniform comprises of a Khadi uniform, black socks, black shoes, a cap and a sports uniform. (Refer to footnote 1)
  • Play school students have to wear the Khadi uniform only during field trips days.
  • During the monsoon, it is mandatory for children to wear all-weather shoes and carry a raincoat in his / her bag.
  • Children are to wear all Black weather shoes with velcro (Action Code - Sr. No. A5)
  • If the children’s uniform does not meet the standards set by the school, he/she will be sent back home
  • There is no sports uniform for Pre-Primary.
  • Black floaters and black socks are not compulsory for Play school and Nursery students.
  • Pinning I-D cards daily is mandatory.

    • LEAVE AND ABSENCE
  • The attendance on the first day & last day of every vacation is mandatory.
  • Leave of absence from school must be obtained a week prior by an application submitted in the prescribed Non-Attendance and Leave record in the diary. The same rules are applicable for half day leave. A child will not be allowed to leave during school hours unless (s)he seeks prior permission, giving valid reasons.
  • A child absent from the school for a period of one month without permission will be struck off the school register. Re-admission into the school will be treated as a fresh application.
  • Play school/Nursery students have to carry an extra pair of clothes and a napkin every day.
  • On the day when there is water play, children of Play School, Nursery & Jr.Kg. have to wear a swimming costume/trunks under their clothes. They must carry a bath towel and an empty plastic bag in their bags.
  • There is no bus service for Play School students.
  • Children attendance mandatory on Sports Day, Annual Day (Including rehearsals) and Project Day.
  • In case a child is absent for more than 3 days a medical certificate must be submitted to the teacher.

Footnote 1
Soak the green khadi pant in salt water before the first wash. Wash the green pant separately to avoid staining. Do not dry any of the uniforms under direct sunlight. Do not use washing material that has high bleaching agent and avoid the use of hot water.

RULES FOR PARENTS
PARENT PARTICIPATION

Building home-school partnership is a best practice because it only helps children adjust and learn easily.

  • It is mandatory for parents to attend all school events such as the School Fair, Sports Day, Annual Day, Project Day and Report Day.
  • It is mandatory to attend Parent Teacher Meetings (PTM's). The students progress will be made known to you at the PTM. On the day of the PTM, the Pre-Primary students will have a holiday.
  • Parent's participation in the field trips is by rotation and is mandatory. Parents will be given the list of days and dates for the trips.
  • The library is open for parents to come with their child, on Saturdays, between 9.00am to 3.00pm (Please check with the office for Library access during vacations.) Please bring your child's identification card / diary along. No eatables or water bottles will be allowed inside the Library. Silence should be maintained at all times. All bags except small money purses should be left at the entrance of the library.
  • Every parent is a member of the Parents Sabha and must participate in the meetings for the same.

COMMUNICATION - FROM SCHOOL AND WITH SCHOOL
  • The student’s information page has to be filled on and submitted on the 2nd day of school.
  • All notices/circulars sent in the child’s diary must be signed by the parents daily.
  • Any queries about your child can be addressed to the class teachers only by way of a note in the diary.
  • The voluntary help form, the parent consent form and the medical report form must be filled in and submitted on the 2nd day of school.
  • If your child is not using the bus service on a particular day, a written note should be sent in the diary to the class teacher.
  • Meeting the Head of Department or any of the teachers requires a prior appointment, requested in writing, through the children’s diary.
  • Parents can avail of the school's counseling service by seeking an appointment with the counselor or special teacher through the class teacher.
  • Cancellation of any of the appointments requires an intimation to theschool office between 8.00am to 9.00am.
  • Parents are expected to notify the school office and class teacher regarding any change in their address or telephone numbers to update our records.
  • Please use the suggestion box freely to drop your suggestion(s), placed outside the School office or in the grievance register in the office or email at pre-primary@shishuvan.com or shishuvanschool@gmail. com
  • The 'Parents Sabha' will be held once a month. The class preparatory meeting will be held one week prior to the ‘Parents Sabha’. The schedule for the same will be sent lat
  • All decisions including some medical decisions, that affect the learning of the student (or due to lack of satisfaction with their academic progress) should be taken in consultation with the school. Any medication administered on the student must be known to the Head of Department, the concerned class teacher and counselor / remedial educator (if involved).
  • Parents will not be allowed to enter the school premises without a valid gate pass. Entry into the school premises for all parents, visitors and outsiders is restricted due to security reasons. Kindly do as instructed by the security personnel at the gate.

RIGHT OF ADMISSION
  • The application for a School Leaving Certificate must be made in writing by the parent or guardian to the School Office. No Leaving Certificate will be issued until all dues are cleared.
  • Management reserves the right to suspend any student from school or from specific school services if behaviour is found inappropriate. The final decision taken by the management would rest on consideration of parent, teacher and administrative viewpoints and would be binding upon the student. Thereafter no discussion would be entertained by the parents.
  • Any decision taken by the school for the welfare of the student and for school discipline will be binding on the parents.
  • Fees must be paid on fine failing which fine will be levied as per school norms. Please not fees once paid is not refunded.
