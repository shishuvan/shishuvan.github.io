Special offer from Gillo for Shishuvan students to watch a children's play at NCPA .

Dear friends at Shishuvan,
Gillo Theatre Repertory would like to make a special offer to the students of Shishuvan for a children's play that is being performed at NCPA on 19th Feb. 2015.
The details of the play are as follows:


Chacha Chhakkan Ke Kaarname. Thurs, 19th February 2015 at 7pm
at Tata Theatre, NCPA
Presented by Pierrot's Troupe, New Delhi
Adapted by Begum Qudsia Zaidi
Directed by Danish Iqbal & Dr. M. Sayeed Alam
Cast: Tom Alter, Niti Phool, Anju Chhabra, Jaskiran Chopra & others
Age: For all above 8 years
Comedy
Language: Hindi
Duration: 1 hr 30 mins (no interval)

Brief synopsis:
Chacha Chhakkan Ke Kaarname is from a series of short plays for children, adapted by Begum Qudsia Zaidi from Imtiaz Ali Taj's short stories. The play revolves around Chacha Chakkan, a forty-five-year-old gentleman, his family, and the quirky misadventures that emerge from his penchant for interfering in household affairs.

The details of the special offer are as follows:
One Ticket Free for every Shishuvan student on purchase of one ticket. (Student must show a valid student ID)
Tickets are priced at Rs.200, Rs.300 and Rs.500/-
These tickets will be available for Shishuvan students as Current Booking only, 1 hour before the performance, at the venue itself. [We have allotted a sufficient number of tickets for this special offer]
We request you to please circulate / communicate details of this offer from Gillo to the parents of Shishuvan students through your mailing list, etc.
[Note: Please DO NOT contact NCPA or Book My Show directly for this special offer]

Gillo Theatre Repertory is a Mumbai based company that produces plays for children and young people.
Gillo is curating the Begum Qudsia Zaidi Centenary Festival in 2014-15, during which 3 plays and an exhibition will be held at NCPA, Mumbai in Feb. 2015.
Begum Qudsia Zaidi, a writer, social worker and theatre practitioner, is best remembered for co-founding the Hindustani Theatre in Delhi. This festival celebrates the work of this important, though forgotten, figure of theatre, including her work for children.

2 other plays (that are not suitable for children) are also part of this festival. Details are in the attached poster. Invitation: H2O - a unique play for toddlers and young children

Announcing H2O by Helios Theatre, Germany a unique play for toddlers and young children, 2 to 8 yrs old Mon 16 Feb | 7 pm | Sitara Studio, Dadar W

Greetings from Junoon!

We are delighted to bring to you, in collaboration with Goethe-Institut/Max-Mueller Bhavan Mumbai and Sitara Studio, a delightful play without words for young children between ages 2 to 8.

The Show H 2 O explores and celebrates the enormous power, sputtering joy and sparkling life of water – as a melting ice-block, shimmering fountain, element of sound, reflector of light. Continuously giving new form to water, the show generates poetry and evokes the wonder of life. And it all starts with a single drop … !

The Group Helios Theatre is a professional group of artists, musicians, puppeteers and players who develop performance for children 2 years up to young people and adults. As part of a larger tour covering Delhi, Karachi, Teheran, Pune, Chennai, Mumbai, Bangalore, Colombo and Dhaka, the team touches down in Mumbai to delight us with their magical show H 2 O, based on water – an element we think we know everything about, but which continues to surprise scientists till date.

The aim of the Reading Challenge is to encourage children to read and enjoy books, which will help them get a head start in life. Mela 2012 - 'Green Economies' (UN Theme) :

This year the SPROUTS Environment Trust's Earth Mela 2012 is going to be held on at the Maharashtra Nature Park Society (MNPS, Dharavi) under the Theme – `Green Economies'. It is a two-day mega-fest comprising a varied range of activities such as a Short-Film Festival, a Photo Exhibition-cum-sale, Workshops and Lecture-Demos, Nature Trails, Bicycle Rally & a Mini-Marathon to stalls of eco-friendly life-style and essential products, an elaborate Adventure Sports Arena and much much more…

Opening on Dec 8 with a Nature Trail (7-9am) at the MNPS & a Bicycle Rally & Mini Marathon (7-9am) on Dec 9, the main event will be from 10am to 6pm on both days.

Advance Registration Fees :Rs. 75 (one day) & Rs. 150 (two days) for Bonafide Students (< 21yrs) and Rs. 150 (one day) & Rs. 300(for two days) for adults.

Spot registration for two days : Rs. 100 (one day) & Rs. 200(two days) for Students and Rs.175 (one day ) & Rs. 350 (two days) for adults.

The Earth Mela has been reaching out to the masses over the last four years with themes such as Indian Forests, Wildlife of India, Biodiversity Conservation & Carbon Reversal Movement. The festival is aimed at providing awareness on nature, biodiversity, eco-friendly lifestyle options, green technologies and conservation actions through responsible decisions.

The Mela has been found relevant and engrossing to participants of all age groups and backgrounds, right from tiny tots to corporate participants. Lectures, Poster Presentations and Workshops by eminent Environmentalists and young scientists will surely engross the adults. And the varied stalls and activities such as adventure sports arena and clay work or collage making by a deaf & mute artist will surely draw in applauds by the younger at heart. The huge variety of stalls will display eco-friendly products and services, such as Paper Mache products, artifacts made by tribal artisans, green technologies, recycling options and organic food stalls, which will help you make better choices in life.

Thus clearly the Mela has interesting games and activities to engage kids and adults alike. The Mela has proven to generate environmentally involved citizens, which is the mandate of the organizers. The photography exhibition and nature trails will tingle your visual, aural and olfactory senses by taking you into regions less seen (forests, deserts, villages) and creatures rarely sighted. Thus helping you gain knowledge and awareness at the same time. The Cycle rally, Mini Marathon and Adventure Sports arena help spread the message of trying out healthier options of transport, vacationing and daring to explore unchartered territories.

The film festival will display a huge array of multi-lingual films dealing with social, cultural, energy, urbanization, wildlife, displacement and educational issues too – giving you an all-round understanding of the multi-dimensional element – we call Our Environment. http://www.facebook.com/events/496895537012098/
